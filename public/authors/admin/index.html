Pedro Baldoni

Postdoctoral Fellow

Walter and Eliza Hall Institute of Medical Research

I am currently a Postdoctoral Fellow at the Walter and Eliza Hall Institute of Medical Research with joint appointment between the Colonial Foundation Healthy Ageing Centre (CFHAC) and the Bioinformatics Division. At WEHI, I develop novel statistical bioinformatic methods for processing, interpreting, and analyzing large-scale proteomic datasets from the latest time-of-flight mass spec technology. I collaborate with the clinical partners of the CFHAC to facilitate biological discoveries related to ageing and disease.

In 2020, I graduated from the PhD program in Biostatistics of the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill. During my doctoral studies, I worked on the field of epigenomics while developing statistical methods for the analysis of high-throughput sequencing epigenomic assays. I have been collaborating in the Hispanic Community Health Study / Study of Latinos (HCHS/SOL) by developing and applying statistical methods to analyze data from the HCHS/SOL under complex survey design settings.
